Sheet 2

This is a puzzle version of the British game show Countdown. In Countdown, there are two types of round: “letters” rounds and “numbers” rounds. In letters rounds, contestants are shown 9 letters and try to make the longest word possible wtih the given letters. In numbers rounds, contestants are shown 6 numbers and are instructed to use arithmetic operations (+, −, ×, ÷) to use these 6 numbers to get as close as possible to a target number. There is also a final “conundrum” round where playesr are presented with a 9-letter anagram that they race to finish first.

In this puzzle, there are far more rounds than usual, and all rounds have unique answers:

For the conundrum rounds, each of the given conundrums has multiple answers. (For instance, CRIMITOES could be EROTICISM or ISOMETRIC.) The correct answer is a synonym of one of the letter rounds answers. The corresponding number round digit can be used as an index into the correct conundrum to get the final answer, NICK OF TIME.
