This is a diagramless criss-cross in braille, with single dot resolution, so frames can be aligned differently and intersections do not necessarily represent the same letter(s) in both words. The “apropos” clues all refer to blind characters. The given numbers are just standard crossword numbering.

That task complete, simply record the areas of the block squares around the perimeter of the puzzle, as hinted by 20 down. For instance, an unused space of area 1 would correspond to A, an unused space of area 20 would correspond to T, and so on. Reading clockwise from the upper right corner, they spell AUSTERE TYPEFACE, which braille certainly is.
