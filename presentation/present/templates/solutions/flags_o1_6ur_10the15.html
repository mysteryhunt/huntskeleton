Six lists have been encoded, as has the title, using a scheme similar to that of standard nautical flags, except that adjacent substitution numbers are summed rather than being represented as standard substitution flags. This is hinted by the puzzle’s substitution flags being in the shape of capital sigmas (Σ). For example, the first five entries in the NATO phonetic alphabet (Alfa, Bravo, Charlie, Delta, Echo) would be encoded as follows:

1
A L F (repeat flag 1)
4
B R (repeat flag 4 (which was itself a repeat of A)) V O
15
C H (repeat flag 7 + rep. 6 + rep. 2 = rep. 15) I E
30
12
D (rep. 16 + rep. 14 = rep. 30) T (rep. 12)
48
(rep. 18 + rep. 10 + rep. 11 + rep. 9 = rep. 48)

(Note that in DELTA, 16 + 14 = 30 refers to the E and L of CHARLIE, and not the 16th and 14th flags, since ARL of CHARLIE is condensed into a single flag.)
