Teams are presented with four photos of locations on MIT’s campus. Each photo has a chalk drawing somewhere within it:

16-2, by the elevators and the Course 20 pset drop box. A large plus sign with a face is drawn on the elevator doors.

6C basement (part of “SPECTROSCOPY” sign in the Bldg 8 basement is visible at end of hallway, as a landmark). A very poorly drawn cat is on the doorframe.

7-1, just off of lobby 7, which is visible through double doors. Two overlapping capital Ws are drawn on one of the room doors.

W20-4th floor, in the center lobby area facing the TCA office. A symbol consisting of a top hat shape and a triangle is on the TCA office door

As clued by the flavor text, these are hobo signs, which historically were drawn with chalk on houses and such to pass messages to following hobos. Each sign used has a particular meaning, and these meanings can be found on sites talking about hobo signs—not all websites detailing hobo signs draw them identically (presumably, neither did the hobos), but some searching should provide the full set of glyph meanings. Each of these photos has been taken directly in front of a bulletin board, whose relevance is suggested by the flavor text (“those behind them”). During Mystery Hunt, each of the bulletin boards had a fake poster. Each poster had a prominent image corresponding the the nearby hobo sign, some text, and a QR code (each image here is linked to the URL in the QR code):

Each of the QR codes links to one fourth of a final image, which is recognizably in the Stata Center, taken from underneath the radar dish in 32-1, near the Dreyfoos entrance. Here is the assembled image:

In the image, the pillar in front has another chalk symbol drawn on it, although this symbol is not recognizable as one of the original hobo signs, and there is no bulletin board nearby:

Using the numbers in the title of the image to re-order the captions provides the cluephrase “NEW_SYMBOLS INSPIRED_BY HOBO_SIGNS CIRCA_2002.”

This sign is associated with “Warchalking”, which is the practice of drawing chalk symbols in public places to advertise the presence of Wifi networks:

The sign in the image means “open Wifi network here,” and the circled section of the symbol represents the SSID of the network.
